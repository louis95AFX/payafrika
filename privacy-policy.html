Privacy Policy

Last Updated: October 1, 2025

1. Introduction

PayAfrika (Pty) Ltd ("PayAfrika", "we", "us", "our") is committed to protecting the privacy and security of your personal information. This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website (payafrika.co.za), use our services, or apply for our products like loans.

We are a registered Credit Provider (NCRCP5164) and are bound by the laws of the Republic of South Africa, including the Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 (POPIA). This policy has been developed to ensure our practices are compliant with POPIA.

2. What Information We Collect

We may collect and process the following types of personal information about you:

  • Identity & Contact Information: This includes your full name, identity number, date of birth, address, email address, and telephone numbers.
  • Financial Information: This includes your bank account details, income information (payslips), credit history, details of your assets and liabilities, and other information necessary for credit assessment and financial services.
  • Transactional Information: Details about payments to and from you, and other details of products and services you have obtained from us.
  • Technical Information: This includes your Internet Protocol (IP) address, browser type and version, time zone setting, browser plug-in types and versions, operating system and platform, and other technology on the devices you use to access our website.
  • Usage Information: Information about how you use our website, products, and services.
  • Communication Information: Information gathered from your communications with us, including interactions via our 24/7 chat line (WhatsApp), email, or phone calls, which may be recorded for quality and training purposes.
  • Marketing and Communications Data: Your preferences in receiving marketing from us and our third parties and your communication preferences.

3. How We Collect Your Information

We use different methods to collect data from and about you, including:

  • Directly from you: When you apply for our products or services (e.g., loans), fill in forms on our website, contact us through our helpline, or correspond with us by post, phone, email, or otherwise.
  • Automated technologies: As you interact with our website, we may automatically collect Technical Data about your equipment, browsing actions, and patterns. We collect this personal data by using cookies and other similar technologies.
  • From third parties: We may receive personal information about you from various third parties, such as credit bureaus (for credit checks), fraud prevention agencies, and other publicly available sources as part of our due diligence (Know Your Customer - KYC) and regulatory obligations.

4. How We Use Your Information

We will only use your personal information when the law allows us to. Most commonly, we will use your personal information in the following circumstances:

  • To process and manage your application for a loan or other financial products.
  • To assess your creditworthiness and affordability.
  • To manage your account with us and to provide you with the services you have requested.
  • To comply with our legal and regulatory obligations, such as Anti-Money Laundering (AML) and Counter-Terrorist Financing (CTF) requirements.
  • To manage our relationship with you, which will include notifying you about changes to our terms or privacy policy.
  • For the detection and prevention of fraud, crime, or other malpractice.
  • To improve our website, products, services, marketing, and customer relationships.
  • Where you have given your consent, to provide you with information about our products and services that may be of interest to you.

5. Disclosure of Your Information

We may share your personal information with the parties set out below for the purposes described in this policy:

  • Credit Bureaus: To conduct credit checks and to report on your repayment history.
  • Service Providers: Third-party companies that provide services to us, such as IT and system administration, hosting, and marketing services.
  • Regulatory and Governmental Authorities: Such as the National Credit Regulator (NCR), the South African Revenue Service (SARS), and other authorities who require reporting of processing activities in certain circumstances.
  • Fraud Prevention Agencies: To prevent and detect fraud.
  • Professional Advisors: Including lawyers, bankers, auditors, and insurers who provide consultancy, banking, legal, insurance, and accounting services.
  • Debt Collection Agencies: If you default on your repayment obligations.

We require all third parties to respect the security of your personal information and to treat it in accordance with the law. We do not allow our third-party service providers to use your personal information for their own purposes and only permit them to process it for specified purposes and in accordance with our instructions.

6. Data Security

We have put in place appropriate technical and organizational security measures to prevent your personal information from being accidentally lost, used, or accessed in an unauthorized way, altered, or disclosed. In addition, we limit access to your personal information to those employees, agents, and contractors who have a business need to know. They will only process your personal information on our instructions, and they are subject to a duty of confidentiality.

7. Data Retention

We will only retain your personal information for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes we collected it for, including for the purposes of satisfying any legal, accounting, or reporting requirements. By law, we have to keep basic information about our customers (including Contact, Identity, Financial, and Transaction Data) for a minimum of five years after they cease being customers for regulatory purposes.

8. Your Legal Rights under POPIA

Under the Protection of Personal Information Act, you have the right to:

  • Request access to your personal information.
  • Request correction of the personal information that we hold about you.
  • Request erasure of your personal information where there is no good reason for us continuing to process it.
  • Object to processing of your personal information where we are relying on a legitimate interest.
  • Object to processing for direct marketing purposes.
  • Request the restriction of processing of your personal information.
  • Lodge a complaint with the Information Regulator of South Africa if you believe we are unlawfully processing your information.

If you wish to exercise any of the rights set out above, please contact our Information Officer at compliance@payafrika.co.za.

9. Cookies

Our website may use cookies to distinguish you from other users. This helps us to provide you with a good experience when you browse our website and also allows us to improve our site. You can set your browser to refuse all or some browser cookies, but note that some parts of this website may become inaccessible or not function properly.

10. Links to Other Websites

Our website may contain links to third-party websites. This Privacy Policy does not apply to those other websites. We recommend you review the privacy policy of any third-party website you visit.

11. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The updated version will be indicated by a "Last Updated" date and will be effective as soon as it is accessible. We encourage you to review this policy frequently to be informed of how we are protecting your information.
